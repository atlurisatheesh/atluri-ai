# LIVE BETA FOUNDER PLAYBOOK
## 14-Day Closed Beta — Human Validation System

**Purpose:** Extract truth, not compliments  
**Audience:** Solo founder running live Zoom sessions  
**Mindset:** You are testing a hypothesis, not demoing a product

---

# PART 1 — EXACT LIVE BETA SESSION SCRIPT

## Pre-Session Setup (Before Zoom Call)

```
□ Test your own mic and camera
□ Have the app open in a browser tab
□ Have a backup notepad for observations
□ Mute Slack/Discord/email
□ Clear your desktop of distractions
□ Have water nearby (you'll be talking)
```

---

## OPENING SCRIPT (Minutes 0-3)

**[When user joins Zoom]**

> "Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. Before we start, I want to be really clear about what this is.
>
> I'm building an AI tool that helps people during interviews. It listens to questions and suggests talking points in real-time.
>
> Today is NOT a demo. I'm not trying to impress you.
>
> I need to find out where this breaks. Where it confuses you. Where it makes things worse instead of better.
>
> You can't hurt my feelings. If something sucks, I need to know. If you hate it, tell me. That's the most valuable thing you can give me.
>
> Any questions before we start?"

**[If they seem nervous]**

> "Also — this isn't a test of you. You're not being evaluated. I'm testing my software. You're helping me find problems. There are no wrong answers."

---

## FRAMING THE TEST (Minutes 3-5)

> "Here's how this will work:
>
> 1. I'll share my screen OR you'll open the app in your browser
> 2. We'll do a mock interview — I'll ask you questions
> 3. The AI will show you suggestions as we go
> 4. Look at them or ignore them — your choice
> 5. After about 15 minutes, we'll stop and talk about what happened
>
> I'm going to take notes during this. I might ask you to pause and tell me what you're thinking. That's normal.
>
> Ready to start?"

---

## DURING THE SESSION

### If Something Breaks

> "Okay, something just happened there. Can you tell me what you saw? What were you expecting?"

**[Don't apologize excessively. Don't explain why it broke. Just note it.]**

> "Got it. Let me write that down. Let's continue."

### If They Seem Confused

> "I noticed you paused there. What were you thinking?"

### If They Ignore Suggestions

> "I saw a suggestion popped up but you didn't look at it. Was that intentional? Were you mid-thought?"

### If They Look Stressed

> "How are you feeling right now? Is the tool helping or adding stress?"

### If System Is Slow

> "That took a while to respond. Did that feel too slow? What would be acceptable?"

---

## CLOSING SCRIPT (Minutes 25-30)

> "Okay, let's stop there. I want to ask you a few quick questions while this is fresh.
>
> First — be honest with me:
>
> 1. **Would you use this in a real interview tomorrow?**
>    Not 'could you' — WOULD you? Yes or no.
>
> 2. **What was the most frustrating moment?**
>    Don't be polite. What annoyed you?
>
> 3. **Did anything make you trust it less?**
>    Any moment where you thought 'this is wrong' or 'I don't believe this'?
>
> 4. **If I gave you this for free forever, would you actually use it?**
>    Or would it sit in your bookmarks and collect dust?
>
> 5. **What's the ONE thing that would make you trust it more?**

**[Write down their exact words. Don't paraphrase.]**

> "Thank you. This was incredibly helpful. I'll be making changes based on this and might reach out for another session in a few days if that's okay.
>
> Any final thoughts?"

---

## POST-SESSION (Immediately After)

Within 5 minutes of hanging up, write down:

```
□ Did they actually use the suggestions?       Y/N
□ Did they seem less stressed with it?         Y/N  
□ Did they trust it?                           Y/N
□ Would they use it in a real interview?       Y/N
□ What broke?                                  [list]
□ What confused them?                          [list]
□ What did they say verbatim about trust?      [quote]
□ My gut feeling: ship / fix / kill            [circle one]
```

# PART 2 — 30-MINUTE LIVE BETA SESSION STRUCTURE

## Minute-by-Minute Breakdown

### Minutes 0-5: Setup + Framing

| Time | Action | Your Script |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 0:00 | User joins | "Hey, thanks for coming" |
| 0:30 | Set expectations | "This is not a demo..." |
| 2:00 | Explain format | "Here's how this works..." |
| 3:30 | Technical setup | "Can you open this link?" |
| 4:30 | Mic check | "Say something so we can test audio" |

**Goal:** Reduce anxiety, establish honesty norm

---

### Minutes 5-10: Warmup Questions (Easy)

| Time | Question | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| 5:00 | "Tell me about yourself" | Test basic flow |
| 7:00 | "What are you most proud of?" | Easy positive question |
| 9:00 | "Why are you interested in [field]?" | Simple to answer |

**Observe:**
- Does suggestion appear?
- How fast?
- Does user look at it?
- Does user seem confused?

**Goal:** Let user get comfortable, verify basics work

---

### Minutes 10-20: Stress Testing (Hard)

| Time | Question | Stress Factor |
|------|----------|---------------|
| 10:00 | "Tell me about a time you failed" | Emotional content |
| 12:00 | "What's your biggest weakness?" | Self-critical thinking |
| 14:00 | "Why should we hire you over others?" | Pressure + sell self |
| 16:00 | "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" | Future uncertainty |
| 18:00 | **[INTERRUPT them mid-answer]** | Simulate rude interviewer |

**Stress Injections:**
```
At 11:00 — Stay silent for 5 seconds after they finish (test awkward pause)
At 13:00 — Say "Can you be more specific?" (increase pressure)
At 15:00 — "I'm not sure I follow..." (create doubt)
At 17:00 — "We're running short on time..." (add urgency)
At 19:00 — Ask a rapid follow-up before they finish
```

**Observe:**
- Does latency increase under pressure?
- Does user stop looking at suggestions?
- Does their voice change?
- Do they seem more stressed WITH the tool?

**Goal:** Find breaking points, stress-test trust

---

### Minutes 20-25: Recovery + Edge Cases

| Time | Action | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| 20:00 | Easy question: "What questions do you have for me?" | Let them recover |
| 21:00 | Go silent for 10 seconds | Test silence handling |
| 22:00 | Ask ambiguous question: "How would you describe yourself in one word?" | Test unusual input |
| 23:00 | Say something unclear: "Mmhmm, interesting..." | Test non-question handling |
| 24:00 | Final question: "Is there anything else you want to share?" | Open-ended |

**Goal:** Test edge cases without breaking rapport

---

### Minutes 25-30: Debrief + Exit Interview

| Time | Question |
|------|----------|
| 25:00 | "Would you use this in a real interview tomorrow?" |
| 26:00 | "What was the most frustrating moment?" |
| 27:00 | "Did anything break your trust?" |
| 28:00 | "One thing that would make you trust it more?" |
| 29:00 | "Any other feedback?" |
| 30:00 | Thank and close |

**Goal:** Extract honest signal while memory is fresh

# PART 3 — SIMULATING 5 NERVOUS CANDIDATES ON ZOOM

## Persona 1: EXTREMELY NERVOUS FRESHER

**Profile:** 22 years old, first job search, hands shaking

### A) Behavior During Session
- Speaks fast then suddenly stops
- Lots of "um", "uh", "like"
- Looks away from camera repeatedly
- Laughs nervously at own answers
- Asks "Is that okay?" after every response
- Voice gets higher when stressed

### B) Where They Get Confused
- "Wait, do I read that out loud?"
- "Should I look at that or at you?"
- "Is it still listening?"
- "Did I do something wrong?" (when system is slow)

### C) Where Trust Drops
- If suggestion appears AFTER they've already started answering
- If suggestion seems generic ("That's not specific to MY experience")
- If system freezes during their most anxious moment
- If they see an error message of any kind

### D) What They Might Say Out Loud
```
"Oh god, okay, okay, let me think..."
"Sorry, I'm so nervous, can I start over?"
"Wait, what was the question again?"
"Is that good? I feel like that was bad."
"The thing showed up but I was already talking."
"I don't know if I should look at it or not."
```

### E) What Would Cause Them to Abandon
- System freezes for more than 3 seconds during stress
- Suggestion is clearly wrong or irrelevant
- They feel MORE nervous with the tool than without
- Any moment where tool makes them feel stupid

### F) Launch Blocking?
**YES — LAUNCH BLOCKING**

This is 40% of your target users. If you can't help nervous people, your product doesn't work.

---

## Persona 2: STRONG ACCENT SPEAKER

**Profile:** 28 years old, Indian accent, fluent but different cadence

### A) Behavior During Session
- Speaks clearly but with distinct pronunciation
- Some words sound different: "develop-ment", "man-ager"
- May repeat themselves if they think you didn't understand
- Slightly formal/polite communication style

### B) Where They Get Confused
- If STT mishears them and suggestion is wrong
- If they have to repeat themselves
- If system seems to "not get" what they're saying

### C) Where Trust Drops
- "Did it understand me?" uncertainty
- When suggestion doesn't match what they said
- If they notice transcription errors

### D) What They Might Say Out Loud
```
"Should I speak more slowly?"
"I think it didn't hear me correctly."
"That's not what I said..."
"Is this working for people with accents?"
"Maybe my pronunciation is the problem."
```

### E) What Would Cause Them to Abandon
- Repeated transcription failures
- Having to "perform" an American accent
- Feeling like tool is "not for them"
- Noticing it works better for native speakers

### F) Launch Blocking?
**YES — LAUNCH BLOCKING**

60%+ of software engineers globally are non-native English speakers. This is a huge market segment.

---

## Persona 3: SLOW SPEAKER WITH PAUSES

**Profile:** 45 years old, senior manager, thinks before speaking

### A) Behavior During Session
- Long (5-10 second) pauses between sentences
- Deliberate, measured responses
- Doesn't use filler words
- May close eyes while thinking

### B) Where They Get Confused
- If suggestion pops up while they're still thinking
- If system "interrupts" their thought process
- If they feel rushed by eager suggestions

### C) Where Trust Drops
- "It thought I was done but I wasn't"
- Multiple suggestions firing before they're ready
- System creating artificial urgency

### D) What They Might Say Out Loud
```
"Hold on, I'm still thinking..."
"Wait, let me finish my thought."
"That's too fast, I wasn't done."
"Can it tell I'm not finished?"
"I need a moment here."
```

### E) What Would Cause Them to Abandon
- Feeling rushed or pressured by the tool
- Suggestions constantly "jumping the gun"
- Tool interrupting their natural rhythm
- Feeling like the tool is for "junior" people

### F) Launch Blocking?
**MEDIUM — Not blocking but limits premium segment**

Senior candidates = higher-paying users. Can ship without, but limits upmarket potential.

---

## Persona 4: FAST OVER-TALKER

**Profile:** 26 years old, ex-sales, talks at 200 words per minute

### A) Behavior During Session
- Never pauses
- Run-on sentences
- Changes topics mid-sentence
- Barely looks at screen — talking to camera
- May not even notice suggestions

### B) Where They Get Confused
- "Where's the suggestion? I didn't see anything."
- "Was it supposed to show something?"
- "Did it work?"

### C) Where Trust Drops
- If they talk for 2 minutes and get nothing
- If suggestion arrives way too late
- If it can't keep up with their pace

### D) What They Might Say Out Loud
```
"Am I going too fast?"
"Should I slow down?"
"I didn't see anything pop up."
"Maybe I should pause more?"
"By the time it showed up I was on something else."
```

### E) What Would Cause Them to Abandon
- Never seeing a timely suggestion
- Feeling like tool can't keep up
- Having to change their natural speaking style
- Getting no value because they're "too fast"

### F) Launch Blocking?
**MEDIUM — Edge case but vocal complainers**

These users will post on Twitter "Tried X, couldn't keep up with me." Can ship without perfect support.

---

## Persona 5: EMOTIONALLY STRESSED CANDIDATE

**Profile:** 38 years old, recently laid off, family financial pressure

### A) Behavior During Session
- Voice cracks occasionally
- Long silences when asked hard questions
- May over-share personal details
- Visible emotional weight

### B) Where They Get Confused
- If suggestion feels tone-deaf to their emotional state
- If tool seems to ignore what they just said
- If it gives "corporate" advice when they're being vulnerable

### C) Where Trust Drops
- Tool showing bullet points when they need empathy
- No acknowledgment of their difficult situation
- Feeling like talking to a cold machine

### D) What They Might Say Out Loud
```
"Sorry, I'm just going through a lot right now."
"This is really important to me."
"I can't afford to mess this up."
"Does it understand what I'm going through?"
"I need help, not bullet points."
```

### E) What Would Cause Them to Abandon
- Tool makes them feel MORE alone
- Suggestions feel generic and impersonal
- No sense that the tool "gets it"
- Making their stress worse, not better

### F) Launch Blocking?
**MEDIUM — Important for retention and word-of-mouth**

These users become your most loyal advocates if you help them. Or your most vocal critics if you don't.

# PART 4 — INTERPRETING EARLY BETA SIGNALS OBJECTIVELY

## The Founder Bias Problem

You will want your product to succeed. That bias will corrupt your interpretation of data.

Rules to follow:

```
1. Write down predictions BEFORE each session
2. Have someone else read feedback verbatim
3. Count behavioral actions, not stated preferences
4. Trust "would" questions, not "could" questions
5. One "I'd never use this" = 10 "this is cool"
```

---

## What Metrics Matter Most (Ranked)

### Tier 1: TRUTH METRICS (Highest Signal)

| Metric | What It Tells You | Red Line |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| "Would use in real interview" (Yes) | Product-market fit | <50% = kill |
| Session completion rate | Core usability | <70% = fix |
| Unsolicited positive statement | Genuine value | 0 in 5 sessions = worry |
| Return for second session | Real interest | <40% = concern |

### Tier 2: TRUST METRICS

| Metric | What It Tells You | Red Line |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| Suggestion follow rate | Trust in AI | <30% = problem |
| Looked at suggestion | Awareness | <50% = UX fail |
| Said "wrong" or "off" | Trust damage | >2 per session = fix |
| Visible stress increase | Net negative | Any = investigate |

### Tier 3: TECHNICAL METRICS

| Metric | What It Tells You | Red Line |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| Response latency | Speed satisfaction | >3s = fix |
| Freezes/errors | Reliability | >1 per session = fix |
| Transcription accuracy | Core function | <85% = fix |

---

## What Early Failures Are NORMAL (Ignore These)

```
✓ "I didn't know where to look" — First 2 sessions
✓ "It took me a minute to understand" — Expected
✓ "The layout is confusing" — UX iteration
✓ User doesn't follow suggestion — Personal preference
✓ Minor latency spikes — Engineering work
✓ Occasional transcription errors — STT limitation
```

---

## What Patterns Indicate PRODUCT-MARKET MISFIT (Danger)

```
✗ "I wouldn't use this even if it worked perfectly"
✗ "I don't see the point"
✗ "This adds more stress, not less"
✗ "I'd rather just practice without it"
✗ Users consistently ignore suggestions (>70%)
✗ No one asks "when can I get this?"
✗ 0/5 users would recommend
```

**If you see these:** Pause beta. Rethink value proposition.

---

## What Patterns Indicate ENGINEERING WEAKNESS

```
✗ Consistent latency >2 seconds
✗ Freezes in >30% of sessions
✗ Transcription fails for specific accents
✗ WebSocket disconnects
✗ Same bug reported by 3+ users
```

**If you see these:** Fix before continuing. Don't collect more polluted data.

---

## What Patterns Indicate TRUST FAILURE (Dangerous)

```
✗ User says "I don't trust it" or "I don't believe it"
✗ User stops looking at suggestions mid-session
✗ User seems MORE anxious with tool than without
✗ User says "It doesn't understand me"
✗ User abandons mid-session (not technical failure)
✗ User gives polite feedback but clearly won't use it
```

**If you see these:** This is existential. Trust is harder to rebuild than features.

---

## What to IGNORE in First 5 Sessions

```
□ UI/UX polish issues
□ Feature requests
□ "It would be cool if..."
□ Minor latency variations
□ Individual quirky behavior
□ Compliments (they're trying to be nice)
```

---

## What Should Trigger IMMEDIATE HOTFIX

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Total crash during session | Stop beta, fix, restart |
| User stress visibly increased | Investigate immediately |
| User says "This is unusable" | Fix before next session |
| Same bug 2+ sessions | Fix before next session |
| 0/3 users would use in real interview | Pause beta, reassess |

---

## GO / NO-GO DECISION FRAMEWORK (After 10 Sessions)

### Calculate These Numbers

```
A = "Would use in real interview" (% Yes)
B = Session completion rate (%)
C = Return for 2nd session (%)
D = Average trust score (0-100)
E = Critical bugs unresolved (count)
```

### Decision Matrix

| Condition | Verdict | Action |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| A≥50%, B≥70%, C≥40%, D≥60, E≤2 | **GO** | Expand to 50 users |
| A≥40%, B≥70%, C≥30%, D≥50, E≤3 | **CONDITIONAL** | Fix issues, retest 5 sessions |
| A<40% OR D<50 OR E>3 | **NO-GO** | Major pivot needed |
| A<30% | **KILL** | Rethink product entirely |

### After 10 Sessions, Ask Yourself:

```
1. Would I bet $100K of my own money that this works?
2. Did anyone say something unsolicited positive?
3. Did anyone ask when they can use it for real?
4. Am I excited to do more sessions, or dreading them?
5. If I showed this to my harshest critic, what would they say?
```

---

## FINAL REALITY CHECK

### Things Founders Tell Themselves (Lies)

```
✗ "They just need to learn how to use it"
✗ "Once we fix X, it'll be fine"
✗ "They said they liked it" (politeness)
✗ "It's just an edge case"
✗ "Technical users will get it"
```

### Things Worth Believing

```
✓ "Would you use this in a real interview tomorrow?" (Yes/No)
✓ They came back for session 2 without being asked
✓ They asked "When is this launching?"
✓ They told a friend about it
✓ They used it when you weren't watching
```
